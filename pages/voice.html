Section 06

Voice.

Direct, simple, human. Like a real, confident woman texting a friend who's about to buy something. Meaning in the first sentence. Not poetic. Not abstract. Not AI-written.

If she'd never text it,
don't send it.

Four pillars

01

Direct, not poetic.

Say what it is, what it does, what to do next. If a normal person wouldn't say it out loud, cut it.

02

Simple, not abstract.

Short sentences. Real words. Name the look, name the set, name what's inside it.

03

Human, not AI.

No mystery numbers like "EST 0042." No filler phrases. Write the way you'd actually talk.

04

Punchy, with personality.

Funny / quirky / sassy / bossy — pick one per piece and use it cleanly. Don't run all four at once.

Tone by audience

The Customer

Direct, simple, human. Close second person. Talk TO her, not about her. Clarity is the new intimacy.

The Wholesale Buyer

Composed, confident, commercial — still recognizably EASTEAM. Lead with proof: best-sellers, sell-through, margin, delivery.

The Email Reader

Every email exists for ONE reason — sell, comment, or testimonial. Pick it before writing. One CTA, no competing buttons.

Cut vs Keep — real examples

The drop announcement

✕ Cut

The chapter boasts a kohl-toned silhouette, uncracked by trend, softer than expected.

✓ Keep

The Daydream Set is back in black. Skirt + top. Drops Friday, 10AM ET.

The reorder ask

✕ Cut

Hello dear, we hope this finds you in a season of softness — perhaps a moment to reflect on a new piece.

✓ Keep

Hey. The skirt you bought just dropped in pink. Want one?

The UGC ask

✕ Cut

We'd love to invite you, should the moment feel right, to share a snippet of your experience.

✓ Keep

Send us a 15-second clip of you in it. We'll share it on our story and send you a $25 credit.

The meaning rule

The meaning of an email, an ad, a caption, or a page must arrive in the first sentence. If she has to read three lines to figure out what you want from her, you've already lost her. Lead with the offer or the ask. Decoration comes after.

The no-dash rule

Never use the long dash, the "—" character, anywhere in EASTEAM copy. It is banned in product descriptions, emails, captions, ads, website text, social, and internal docs alike. Internal or external, it does not belong in our writing. Rewrite the sentence with a period, a comma, or a colon instead, or split it in two. The one exception is data we do not author: a Shopify product name or an imported field may already contain one, and that is data, not voice.

Notification email voice

The transactional emails follow all of the above, plus these specifics (full build rules live on the Notification Emails page).

Open with "Hey [name]."

Refund emails lead with "Your refund is on the way." — never remind her in the headline that it's store credit. Say "total refund," never hardcode the amount.

Hard moments (canceled, declined) start with empathy, then the facts.

No false promises — don't offer personal replies, DMs back, or help we won't actually give.

Always delete the customs/duties closing paragraph that ships in the OrderlyEmails base. No dashes in our copy (Shopify product names may contain them — that's data, not voice).
