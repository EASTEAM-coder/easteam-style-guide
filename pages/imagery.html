Lighting — the only on-set rule that matters

  • Light quality: soft, natural light. Avoid harsh shadows, blown highlights, or overly bright areas that distort fabric.
  • Setup: minimum two sources — one front light to even the subject, one side or back light for depth and contour.
  • Temperature: consistent across all lights. No mismatched tones, no post-production fixes.
  • Color accuracy: non-negotiable. Skirt yellow on camera must equal the real skirt yellow. Pull the linesheet if unsure.
  • Goal: show fabric, drape, and silhouette at their most refined — never let the lighting upstage the garment.
The garment is the subject. Not the light.
